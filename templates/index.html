Frequently Asked Questions

Air passenger rights are legal protections that ensure travelers receive compensation and assistance in cases of flight delays, cancellations, or overbooking caused by airlines.
Eligibility for compensation depends on the length of the delay, the cause, and the regulations of the country you are traveling in or from. Generally, if your flight is delayed by more than 3 hours due to reasons within the airline's control, you may be entitled to compensation.
Compensation amounts vary based on factors such as the duration of the delay and the distance of the flight. For example, in the European Union, compensation can range from €250 to €600.
The time limit for filing a claim varies by country and airline. In the EU, you typically have up to three years to file a claim, while in the US, it can range from one to three years depending on the state.
If your flight is canceled, you may be entitled to a full refund or an alternative flight. Additionally, if the cancellation occurs within 14 days of your scheduled departure, you may also be eligible for compensation, depending on the reason for the cancellation.
